# Official Data Sources

Use official sources as primary sources. Third-party legal databases can be useful for comparison, but they should not be the canonical source for this project.

## Statutes

Source: India Code  
Use for: Central Acts, State Acts, sections, amendments, PDFs.

Priority Acts:

- Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023
- Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023
- Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam, 2023
- Indian Penal Code, 1860
- Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973
- Indian Evidence Act, 1872
- Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881
- Information Technology Act, 2000
- Consumer Protection Act, 2019
- Hindu Marriage Act, 1955
- Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005
- Transfer of Property Act, 1882
- Constitution of India
- Motor Vehicles Act, 1988
- Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

## Gazette

Source: e-Gazette of India and state gazettes  
Use for: commencement notifications, amendments, repeals, rules, regulations and delegated legislation.

Gazette is critical because an Act can exist before all provisions are in force.

## Supreme Court

Sources:

- Supreme Court of India website
- e-SCR search
- DigiSCR for older reports

Use for: authoritative precedent, neutral citations, coram, landmark judgments and cited-case graph.

## High Courts

Sources:

- DOJ Judgment Search Portal
- Individual High Court websites where required

Priority High Courts:

- Delhi
- Bombay
- Madras
- Allahabad
- Karnataka
- Calcutta

## District Courts

Source: eCourts Services  
Use for: case metadata, public orders and judgments.

Do not assume district court portals provide complete case files. Pleadings, evidence bundles, witness statements and FIR copies are usually not publicly available through eCourts.

## NJDG

Source: National Judicial Data Grid  
Use for: aggregate judicial metrics and court-level context. Treat it as a statistics source, not a case-file source.
